MTD (Making Tax Digital)

In 2015, 85% of self-assessment tax forms within the UK were completed online. It was also in this year that the UK government outlined their plans to ensure all self-assessment forms were completed digitally. To facilitate this change, from April 2022 all VAT-registered businesses that were previously under the turnover threshold (£85,000) will be legally required to complete self-assessment tax forms digitally as part of the Making Tax Digital (MTD) Government scheme. However, this policy does not apply to smaller VAT-registered businesses with turnover less than £10,000.

Self Assessment Tax Forms

The base form for Self-Assessment in the United Kingdom is the SA100. The SA100 covers the consistent information that applies to all areas of taxation. The SA100 form includes several personal and business financial details, such as: Gross and net taxable profits and pensions or state benefits. All SA100 sections fulfils the basic business data that applies to all businesses. However, there are supplementary forms that are also required to be filled in based on current job or financial status.

Supplementary Self Assessment Tax Forms

There are two supplementary forms in addition to SA100 for the self-employed: the SA103S for individuals whose turnover is less than £85,000, and the SA103F for turnover exceeding £85,000.

VAT (Value Added Tax)

VAT is a consumption taxation on goods and services. This taxation is collected by a government selected third party but is regulated through government control and polices. The current VAT rate in Scotland is set at 15% however, due to the economic impact of the Covid-19 government issued lockdown, this rate has been reduced temporarily for certain areas of goods and services

Income Tax

Income taxation is where a percentage of a person’s wage is paid to the government to fund public sectors. The percentage is determined by the wage bracket.

Brexit

When the UK leaves the EU VAT area, it will become a third country. This means that the way businesses manage VAT on goods and services exported and imported to/from the EU will change. Sellers will not charge VAT, but buyers will have to pay VAT to HMRC at the point of import (alongside any applicable customs duties).

Covid-19

On 8 July 2020, the government announced that it would introduce a temporary 5% reduced rate of VAT for certain supplies of hospitality, hotel and holiday accommodation, and admissions to certain attractions. The Chancellor of the Exchequer announced on 24 September 2020 that the temporary reduced rate for tourism and hospitality will be extended to 31 March 2021.

Invoices & Quotes

When a customer pays your invoice, you need to apply their payment to it. This changes the status of the invoice from Outstanding to Paid, or Part Paid if the customer hasn’t paid you the full amount yet.

A quote is an exact price for a job that you agree with your customer before starting the work. Your quote should specify precisely the amount and type of work it covers, and the relevant costs.
